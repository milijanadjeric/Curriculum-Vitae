About Me

I was born in Bihac (Bosnia and Herzegovina). I lived in Korenica (Croatia), until 1991. In 2001 I started with studies of philosophy at The Faculty of Philosophy in Belgrade/Serbia, and I graduated in June 2008 - Bachelor of Philosophy. At present, in addition to being a Research Assistant at the Department of Philosophy - The Faculty of Philosophy in Belgrade, I am engaged in scientific project “Dynamic Systems in Nature and Society: Philosophical and Empirical Aspects” as a Research Assistant (Project Carrier: The Institute for Philosophy at the University of Belgrade, Project Funding: The Republic of Serbia, Ministry of Education and Science). I am also a member of Secretariat at The Serbian Unit of the International Network of The UNESCO Chair in Bioethics. My research areas are: Philosophical Ethics, Applied Ethics, Bioethics – various topic areas including abortion, euthanasia and those linked to use of biotechnology: genetic engineering, cloning, stem cells research.
